Basic Concept

Microeconomics is a study of how individuals, households, and firms make decisions and allocate resources. For example, whether a high school graduate chooses to go to college or directly into the workforce is a microeconomic decision.(Opportunity cost)

Economic is about 5 key components: Scarcity, Resources, Choices, Three essential economic questions and Economic Systems.

Scarcity

Scarcity is the fundamental problem. Humanity don't have everything we want. For example, you wanted the nike good shoe, but it's already sold out. Scarcity is our inability of having unlimited resources to satisfy everyone. If the product is scarce, we can estimate that the product has a positive price, less avaliable than wanted and having opportunity cost.

The factors of production: Land, Labor, Human Capital and Entrepreneurship. These are required to create products through production. (entrepreneurship means a person or a firm taking a risk to gain profit in the market)

Economic Systems

Basic Economic Questions:

1. What are you gonna produce? There is limited amount of resourses within scarcity, it should be cafually chosen to know what to produce by using those limited amount of resourses. This question helps the firm to think which would the product be helpful for people in the market. For example, if there's a lot of water bottle companies, you don't have to go for a water firm.

2. How to produce? You could choose to put more human capital onto machines rather than human labor or vice versa. This question helps the firm to produce the product more efficiently.


3. For whom to produce? Who gets the products? How will it be distributed out in the market? This question will help the firm to create an allocatively efficient production.


Type of Economies:

Command Economy The central planners choose deciding the three basic economic questions, you could see this economy in North Korea.


Market Economy Individuals in the society decide whether to buy or sell within the market. The individuals decide the three basic economic questions.


Opportunity Cost


The opportunity cost is the value of cost that the next best alternative choice was not chosen. It's the cost of choice, not being able to choose the next best choice. For example, you can go into university after a graduation of highschool, then the opportunity cost of that choice could be like working and gaining salary rather than going to the university.


Two Types of cost

Explicit cost: It's the money that cost in a money wise. The cost that you pay with human capital.


Implicit cost: It's opportunity cost, that you lose from making a choice.


For example, if a person was working at a superstore for $400 per day and quit that job to create his own superstore by paying $200 and makes $500 in revenue per day.


The Explicit cost of creating superstore would be $200 and the implicit cost would be $400 in this way. So, if you compare this with these costs to the revenue that are made, it makes an accounting profit, but makes a loss according to the economic profit.
